Facts About Elephants

  • Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth.
  • There are two species of elephants: African elephants and Asian elephants.
  • Elephants have long trunks that they use for breathing, smelling, drinking, and grasping objects.
  • Elephants are highly social animals and live in groups called herds.
  • Female elephants, called cows, typically live in family groups led by the oldest and largest female, known as the matriarch.
  • Elephants have a lifespan of up to 70 years in the wild.
  • Despite their size, elephants are capable of running at speeds of up to 25 miles per hour.
