Did you know that foxes belong to the Canidae family, which also includes wolves and domestic dogs?

Foxes are highly adaptable animals and can thrive in a variety of environments, from forests and grasslands to urban areas.

There are about 37 species of foxes found around the world, including the red fox, Arctic fox, and fennec fox.

Foxes are known for their intelligence and cunning behavior. They are skilled hunters and have been known to use their intelligence to outwit prey.

Many fox species are nocturnal, meaning they are most active at night.

Female foxes are called vixens, and they typically give birth to litters of 4 to 6 pups.
