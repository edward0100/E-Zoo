Facts about Spiders
  • Spiders are arachnids, not insects. They have eight legs and two body segments: the cephalothorax and the abdomen.
  • There are over 45,000 known species of spiders, found on every continent except Antarctica.
  • Spiders are carnivorous and primarily feed on insects, using venom to immobilize their prey.
  • Most spiders are harmless to humans, but some species have venom that can cause painful bites or allergic reactions.
  • Spiders produce silk from specialized glands located in their abdomen. They use silk to build webs for catching prey, create egg sacs, and construct shelters.
  • Many spider species exhibit complex mating behaviors, including courtship rituals and elaborate displays.
  • Female spiders are often larger than males and may cannibalize their mates after mating.
