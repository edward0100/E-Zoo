Facts about Snakes.
  • Snakes are elongated, legless reptiles that belong to the suborder Serpentes.
  • There are over 3,000 known species of snakes, found on every continent except Antarctica.
  • Snakes have flexible jaws and can swallow prey much larger than their own head, thanks to their ability to dislocate their jawbones.
  • They are carnivorous and primarily feed on rodents, birds, eggs, and other small animals.
  • Snakes use their forked tongues to "taste" the air and detect scent particles, helping them locate prey and navigate their environment.
  • Many snake species are venomous and use venom to immobilize or kill their prey. However, not all snakes are venomous, and some species rely on constriction to subdue their prey.
  • Snakes play important roles in ecosystems as both predators and prey. They help control populations of rodents and other small animals.
