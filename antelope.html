Interesting Facts About Antelopes

Did you know that antelopes are a diverse group of herbivorous mammals found primarily in Africa and parts of Asia?

Antelopes are known for their agility and speed, which helps them evade predators such as lions and cheetahs.

There are over 90 species of antelopes, ranging in size from the tiny royal antelope to the large eland.

Many antelope species form herds for protection and foraging. These herds can range in size from a few individuals to thousands.

Antelopes have excellent senses, including keen eyesight and hearing, which help them detect predators and other dangers.

Antelopes play a crucial role in their ecosystems by grazing on vegetation, which helps maintain the balance of plant populations.
