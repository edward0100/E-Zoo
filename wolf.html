  • Wolves are highly social animals and live in packs, which typically consist of a mated pair and their offspring.
  • They communicate through howls, barks, growls, and body language.
  • Wolves are skilled hunters and primarily prey on large herbivores such as deer, elk, and moose.
  • They are found in a variety of habitats, including forests, tundra, and grasslands.
  • Wolves are known for their intelligence, adaptability, and complex social structures.
  • They play a crucial role in maintaining healthy ecosystems by controlling prey populations.
  • Despite their fearsome reputation, wolves generally avoid conflicts with humans and will only attack if threatened or provoked.
